üîç Surprising & Counterintuitive Findings

‚ö°

No Sandbox Participants Announced Yet

Despite sandbox launching July 1, 2025, no participants have been publicly disclosed as of September 30, 2025. This creates a first-mover advantage for MoMo to shape regulatory guidance and establish market leadership before competitors like VNPAY or ZaloPay join.

üåç

No Cross-Border ASEAN Integration (Yet)

Sandbox is strictly Vietnam-territory with no cross-border P2P or Open API linkages to ASEAN partners initially. Future frameworks may emerge post-sandbox (up to 4 years), but current focus is domestic. Limits immediate regional expansion but reduces regulatory complexity.

üö´

Credit Institutions Barred from P2P

Traditional banks and foreign branches cannot provide P2P lending solutions (Decree 94, Art. 3). This exclusion favors non-bank fintechs like MoMo, reducing incumbent competition and enabling innovation in alternative lending for SMEs and underbanked segments.

üìà

Biometric Fraud Spike During Rollout

Vietnam's 2024 biometric mandate saw increased fraud via phishing scams (scammers posing as bank staff) and high-res photo/video bypasses. This underscores need for robust Presentation Attack Detection (PAD) like ISO Level 2 certification, not just basic biometric checks.

Ready to Seize the Sandbox Opportunity?

Vietnam's regulatory sandbox presents a time-sensitive opportunity to innovate in P2P lending and Open API integrations. MoMo's early participation can secure market leadership in a projected $76B digital finance sector by 2030.
