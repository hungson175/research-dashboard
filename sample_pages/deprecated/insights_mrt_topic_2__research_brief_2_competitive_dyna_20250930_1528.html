Competitive Threats Analysis

üõí

1. Super-App Bundling Erosion

Threat: Grab (55% ride-hailing share) and ShopeePay (66.7% e-commerce) leverage ecosystem bundling‚Äîride-hailing + food + payments or shopping + BNPL‚Äîcreating stickiness that erodes standalone wallet usage.

Impact: Moca's July 2024 shutdown shifted users to MoMo/ZaloPay, but super-apps now directly compete via embedded finance. BNPL market growing 36.5% in 2025 to $2.61B‚Äîplayers like Grab-MoMo partnerships blur lines.

Evidence: Shopee's 350,000+ Vietnamese SMEs exported via SIP; GrabPay integrates PayLater (BNPL) with 3X TPV post-launch. Urban hubs (HCMC/Hanoi) contribute 45% of transactions‚Äîprime battlegrounds.

üîÄ

2. QR Interoperability Fragmentation Risk

Threat: Vietnam's VietQR is non-mandatory, leading to fragmented codes (merchant vs. transfer QR confusion). Cross-border QR with Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, China effective 2025, but weak merchant regulations and security risks (fraud-prone merchant-presented mode) limit inbound transactions.

Risk: If MoMo doesn't lead VietQR standardization and cross-border integration, competitors like VNPAY (750,000+ merchants) or ZaloPay (57% QR share claim via Zalo integration) capture regional flows, eroding MoMo's 55.41% QR mode share.

Context: QR market to hit $523M by 2032 (14.27% CAGR). NAPAS processes 26M daily transactions; cross-border expansion to Japan/South Korea planned.

3. Bank-Direct VietQR Push

Threat: Major state banks (Vietcombank, BIDV, Agribank) pushing direct VietQR in apps, bypassing e-wallets. Post-Moca shutdown, ride-hailing payments shifted to MoMo/ZaloPay and bank apps.

Impact: 86.97% adult bank account penetration (2024) creates ready alternative. If banks enhance UX (e.g., BIDV's VietQRCash ATM withdrawals), MoMo's convenience edge narrows.
